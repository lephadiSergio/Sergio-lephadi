I am a final-year BSc (Hons) Electrical Engineering student at the University of the Witwatersrand in Johannesburg, graduating in 2026. My degree spans power systems, high-voltage engineering, control, instrumentation, signal processing, and systems management — a deliberately broad foundation for solving complex infrastructure challenges.

Beyond the lecture hall, I have led. As EIE Student Council Chairperson, I represented over 200 students and worked to bridge the gap between student experience and institutional governance. As Treasurer of the Faculty of Engineering and Built Environment Student Council, I gained practical exposure to financial stewardship in an institutional setting.

"The best engineers I have seen are the ones who understand that every technical system is embedded in a human one."

My interest in renewable energy — particularly solar — grew through self-directed study alongside formal coursework in power and high-voltage engineering. I have also participated in the FirstRand START Quant Summer School, which deepened my appreciation for quantitative modelling and data-driven decision-making.

I am open to relocating within South Africa and am actively seeking graduate opportunities in power, energy, and electrical engineering where I can contribute meaningfully from day one.
