My Journey

From Lufhereng to Wits

I grew up in Lufhereng, a developing township on the western edge of Soweto, Johannesburg. Lufhereng is one of Johannesburg's largest housing developments, created to provide opportunities for thousands of families. Growing up in this environment taught me resilience, adaptability, and the importance of creating opportunities where they may not yet exist.

As the eldest of four siblings and the first person in my family to complete an engineering degree, I have always understood that success is rarely achieved alone. My journey has been shaped by family, mentors, teachers, and a community that encouraged me to dream beyond my circumstances.

Today, I am completing a BSc (Hons) in Electrical Engineering at the University of the Witwatersrand, and I continue pursuing opportunities to learn, lead, and contribute meaningfully.

What's In A Name?

Growing up, older members of my community often called me by completely different names. Sometimes it was Samkelo, other times completely different names altogether. For a while, it was slightly frustrating 🙃.

But as I grew older, I realised something interesting. Although the names were different, many of them carried meanings connected to hope, perseverance, gratitude, and leadership.

Looking back, those experiences taught me that identity is shaped not only by the name we carry, but also by the values we live by and the impact we leave on others. Whether people know me as Sergio, student, engineer, mentor, or leader, I hope each title reflects positive contribution and meaningful service.
